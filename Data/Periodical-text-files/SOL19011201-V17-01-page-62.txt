WHERE IS THE DIFFERENCE ? A Seventeenth-Century Court Scene Thomas Gould, a Baptist, being admonished by the judge of the county court at Charlestown, Mass., in October, 1656. Gould had refused to have his baby sprinkled and christened at church, because he "lookt upon it as no ordi-nance of Christ." He was convicted of "denying baptism to his child."
